## What is it

An **EIN** (Employer Identification Number) is a 9-digit number the IRS assigns to a business so it can be identified for tax purposes. Think of it as the "social security number" of your business.

## When do I need one

- To open a business bank account.
- To hire employees.
- To file business taxes (federal and state).
- To apply for business licenses or permits.
- To set up payroll.

## How to get it

There are two main paths:

1. **Online through the IRS** — free, takes ~15 minutes, available only if you have a US Social Security Number or ITIN.
2. **Through a third party** (like us) — needed if you don't have an SSN/ITIN, or if you want to avoid the IRS website.

> **Tip:** if you're a non-US founder without an ITIN, applying via Form SS-4 by fax/mail can take 4-6 weeks. A service that does it for you typically gets the EIN in 1-2 business days.

## Common mistakes

- **Confusing EIN with ITIN.** EIN is for businesses, ITIN is for individuals without SSN.
- **Getting an EIN before forming the LLC.** Wrong order — you need the business entity first, then the EIN.
- **Losing the EIN letter.** The IRS only sends it once. Save the PDF.
