Forming a Florida LLC takes about 1 business day once your application is in. This is the complete step-by-step:

## Step 1 — Pick 3 business names

Florida requires the name to be unique. Pick **3 candidates** in order of preference — if the first one is taken, the State automatically rejects without explanation, so giving 3 saves rounds.

Rules:
- Must end with "LLC", "L.L.C." or "Limited Liability Company".
- Can't be identical or confusingly similar to an existing entity.
- No restricted words (Bank, Trust, etc.) unless you have a license.

## Step 2 — Pick a Registered Agent

The RA is the person/company that legally receives lawsuits and government notices on behalf of the LLC. Two options:

| Option | Cost | When it makes sense |
|---|---|---|
| Use a service | ~$100-150/year | You don't have a Florida address, or you don't want your home address public. |
| Be your own RA | Free | You live in Florida, have a physical address (not P.O. Box), and accept that the address goes in public records. |

## Step 3 — File Articles of Organization

This is the document that creates the LLC. It needs:
- Company name (one of your 3 picks).
- Registered Agent name + Florida address.
- Members or Managers (people who own/run the business).
- Principal place of business.

Filing fee: **$125** (state filing fee, paid directly to Florida Department of State).

## Step 4 — Wait for approval

Florida typically processes filings within **3-5 business days** (standard) or **1-2 business days** (expedited — extra fee).

When approved, you receive a **Certificate of Formation** — keep this safe, you'll need it for banks and contracts.

## Step 5 — Get your EIN

Now that the LLC exists, apply for the EIN with the IRS. See the `ein-tax-id` article in our wiki for details.

## Step 6 — Open business bank account

Take your Certificate of Formation + EIN to any major bank. You'll need:
- Photo ID of all signers.
- Operating Agreement (if any).
- Initial deposit (usually $25-$100).
