An LLC (Limited Liability Company) offers flexible management and pass-through taxation — ideal for small businesses and entrepreneurs. A Corporation is a more formal structure suited for businesses seeking investors or planning to issue stock. Both protect your personal assets from business liabilities. Not sure which is right for you? Contact us and we'll help you decide.
Standard processing with the Florida Division of Corporations typically takes 7–10 business days once we submit your documents. With our Expedited Filing service (included free in Premium, or available as an add-on for Basic/Standard), processing can be reduced to 1–3 business days. We'll keep you updated every step of the way.
Yes — every LLC and Corporation in Florida is legally required to have a Registered Agent with a physical street address in the state. The Registered Agent receives official legal documents, tax notices, and service of process on behalf of your business. We offer this service so you never miss a critical legal notice.
While a single-member LLC can technically use the owner's Social Security Number for tax purposes, obtaining an EIN (Employer Identification Number) is strongly recommended. Banks require it to open a business account, and it protects your personal SSN from being shared with clients and vendors.
Absolutely. There are no residency requirements to form an LLC or Corporation in Florida. Non-U.S. residents can form a Florida business entity. You will need a Florida Registered Agent (which we provide) and may also need an ITIN if you don't have a Social Security Number — a service we also offer.
An Operating Agreement is an internal document that defines the rules of your LLC: ownership percentages, member roles, voting rights, and how decisions are made. Most banks require it — alongside your EIN and Certificate of Formation — to open a business checking account. We prepare this document as part of our Standard and Premium packages.
